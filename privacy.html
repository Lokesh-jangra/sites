How We Use Your Data

We process your personal information solely for legitimate business purposes and in accordance with applicable laws and regulations. Specifically, we use your data to:

  • Provide personalised wealth management, investment advisory, and financial planning services
  • Develop comprehensive financial plans, investment strategies, and portfolio recommendations
  • Execute transactions, manage accounts, and administer your portfolio
  • Conduct know-your-client (KYC) and anti-money laundering (AML) compliance procedures
  • Communicate with you regarding your accounts, market insights, and service updates
  • Fulfil our regulatory reporting and record-keeping obligations
  • Improve our services, website functionality, and client experience
  • Protect against fraud, unauthorised activity, and legal claims

We will never sell your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes. Your data is treated as strictly confidential and is handled with the same care and discretion we apply to managing your wealth.

Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may share your data only in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service Providers: With trusted third-party vendors who assist us in delivering our services, including custodians, technology providers, and compliance consultants. These parties are contractually bound to protect your information and use it only for the purposes we specify.
  • Legal & Regulatory: When required by law, regulation, subpoena, court order, or governmental authority. We comply fully with requests from the SEC, FINRA, and other regulatory bodies with jurisdiction over our operations.
  • Professional Advisors: With your legal counsel, tax advisors, or accountants when you have authorised us to coordinate with them on your behalf.
  • Business Transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of the transaction. We will provide notice before your data becomes subject to a different privacy policy.
  • With Your Consent: In any other circumstance where you have provided explicit, informed consent for us to share your information.

Data Security

We employ industry-leading administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect your personal information from unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. Our security measures include:

  • AES-256 encryption for data in transit and at rest
  • Multi-factor authentication for all systems containing client data
  • Regular penetration testing and vulnerability assessments by independent security firms
  • Role-based access controls ensuring employees access only the data necessary for their responsibilities
  • Comprehensive employee training on data privacy and information security best practices
  • SOC 2 Type II audited infrastructure and business continuity planning
  • Physical security measures at all office locations including access controls and surveillance

While we take extensive measures to protect your information, no method of electronic storage or transmission is completely secure. We continuously review and update our security practices to address emerging threats and maintain the highest standards of data protection.

Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of Access: You may request a copy of the personal information we hold about you, along with details about how it is being processed.
  • Right to Rectification: You may request that we correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal information.
  • Right to Erasure: Subject to regulatory retention requirements, you may request that we delete your personal information when it is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected.
  • Right to Restrict Processing: You may request that we limit the processing of your personal information in certain circumstances.
  • Right to Data Portability: You may request a structured, machine-readable copy of your personal information for transfer to another service provider.
  • Right to Object: You may object to our processing of your personal information for direct marketing purposes at any time.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Where processing is based on your consent, you may withdraw that consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact our Privacy Officer at privacy@tuckerhamberg.com or by calling +1 (234) 567-890. We will respond to all legitimate requests within 30 business days.

Cookies Policy

Our website uses cookies and similar technologies to enhance your browsing experience, analyse site traffic, and understand user behaviour. Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit our website.

Types of Cookies We Use

  • Strictly Necessary Cookies: Essential for the operation of our website. These cookies enable core functionality such as page navigation, secure area access, and form submissions. The site cannot function properly without them.
  • Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. We use this data to improve site performance and content relevance.
  • Functional Cookies: Allow the website to remember choices you make, such as your preferred language or region, and provide enhanced, personalised features.
  • Performance Cookies: Collect information about how you use our website, such as which pages you visit most often and whether you encounter error messages. These cookies do not collect personally identifiable information.

Managing Cookies

You can control and manage cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to refuse or delete cookies. Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website. For more information about managing cookies, visit your browser's help documentation or allaboutcookies.org.
